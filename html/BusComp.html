Business Competition Experiences

Business competitions are one of my favorite ways to learn because they require intensive research, strategic thinking, and effective communication. They help me practice building a business model, presenting with confidence, and improving through feedback.

How I Approach Competitions (Process)

  1. Define the Problem and Solution
    • Identify the pain point clearly
    • Validate the target user/customer
    • Define a solution that is realistic and scalable
  2. Build the Business Model
    • Revenue model and cost assumptions
    • Competitive differentiation
    • Go-to-market strategy and milestones
  3. Pitch and Improve
    • Structure the story (problem → solution → traction)
    • Design simple and readable slides
    • Practice Q&A and iterate using feedback
Key Strengths
Critical thinking, teamwork, attention to detail, communication
What I Practice
Pitch clarity, financial assumptions, storytelling, Q&A
Main Takeaway
Better decision-making under pressure through structured preparation

The first business competition I participated in was because of a business class project. Our professor challenged us to send our business plans to the competition and see how far we can go. My teamate and I were named finalists!

My third business competition where I won third place. This was when I was presenting in front of a panel of business entrepreneurs. Fun fact, my business was the only one in the actual planning stage that won. The rest were either startups or had secured contracts.
